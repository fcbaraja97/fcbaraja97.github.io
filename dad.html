Francisco Neri

My dad is someone who I never thought I'd relate too. He's a bit more calous than me and that is because he had a very hard life. From leaving his home country of Mexico to come the United States for better opportunity and to be with the rest of his family, to working day in and day out on scolding hot roofs to provide for his family. Things haven't always been the best between us but as I go further in life I realize that there is no instruction manual to this. We all make the best of it and I never gave you that but here and now I'll say, I'm sorry.

Interests

Soccer

For as long as I can remember my dad loved soccer. He loved watching it, hearing it and especially playing it. My dad had such a mind for the sport that he coached a team to a championship in soccer. If things had been different for him I believe my dad would've become a professional soccer player.

Stand up comedy

One day, just out of the blue, my dad begins to play music from his phone but what came out wasn't his normal tunes but segments of stand up comedy. Turns out my dad enjoys a good laugh as much as the next guy *badum-tss*. It was just so odd because my dad has never been the guy to laugh at everything but after he found out about a few comedians it's been one of his go-to playlist when he works.

Music

I get a lot of my musical taste from my dad. He enjoys Mexican music and country the most. His favorite artist is Johnny Cash and one song that always reminds me of my dad and that is 'a boy named sue'. My dad showed me that song for the first time when I was a kid and something about just reminds me of him each I go to listen to the record.
